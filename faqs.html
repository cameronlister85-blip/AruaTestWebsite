Support

Frequently asked questions.

arua.co.nz  ·  Updated: 14 May 2026

Answers to the most common questions about how Arua works, who it's for, how your medication is handled, and what we do with your information. If something isn't covered here, get in touch at hello@arua.co.nz.

General

Arua is a New Zealand men's health service. We make it straightforward to get assessed for common men's health conditions — like erectile dysfunction and hair loss — and, if treatment is appropriate, to have medication prescribed and delivered to your door. The whole thing is designed to be done from your phone, in your own time, without sitting in a waiting room.

Men over 18 living in New Zealand. If you're outside NZ, or under 18, we're not the right fit.

No. Arua is built to handle specific conditions well, not to replace the broader care your GP provides. If you have complex health issues, ongoing conditions that need active management, or anything that needs a hands-on physical examination, your GP is still the right call. For the conditions we treat, we're a faster and more private alternative — but we're an alternative, not a replacement.

How it works

You create an account and complete a health questionnaire about the condition you're seeking help for. A clinician reviews your answers and, if you're suitable for treatment, you'll have a telehealth consult with a New Zealand-registered GP. If they prescribe, your medication is dispensed by our pharmacy partner and delivered to your door. Four steps: sign up, questionnaire, consult, delivery.

It's a one-on-one video consult with a registered GP, done remotely so you can do it from wherever suits — home, car, lunch break. The clinician will already have read your questionnaire, so the consult isn't about repeating yourself. It's about them verifying what you've told them, checking for anything the questionnaire couldn't capture, answering your questions, and making the prescribing decision.

The questionnaire takes most men 5 to 10 minutes. Clinician review and consult booking typically happen within one to two business days. Once your consult is done and a prescription is issued, our goal is to have your medication at your door within 48 hours.

Every clinician working through Arua is registered with the relevant New Zealand professional body — the Medical Council of New Zealand or the Pharmacy Council of New Zealand — and licensed to prescribe in this country. They're not offshore. They're not AI. They're New Zealand-registered professionals operating under the same standards they would in any GP clinic.

A human clinician reviews every assessment, and a human GP runs every consult. Software helps us route information to the right clinician and flag things that need attention, but the clinical decision — whether to prescribe, what to prescribe, what dose — is always made by a registered prescriber during a one-on-one consult with you.

No. Clinicians work independently. They assess you against their professional judgment and standard prescribing guidelines, and they make the call. Arua provides the platform that connects you to them and handles the operational side — we don't influence or override what they decide. If a clinician declines to prescribe, that's their decision, and we respect it.

They'll message you through your account, or arrange a follow-up consult if it warrants one. You'll get an email letting you know there's something waiting. Until you respond, your assessment sits paused.

The clinician will explain why during your consult, or in a message afterwards if more thinking is needed. Sometimes it's because treatment isn't appropriate for what you've described. Sometimes it's because more information is needed than a remote consult can capture, and you'd be better served by an in-person appointment with a GP who can examine you. Either way, you'll get a clear explanation and, where relevant, a steer on what to do next.

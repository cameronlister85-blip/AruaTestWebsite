Hair loss.

Male pattern hair loss is progressive. Starting treatment earlier produces significantly better outcomes. A NZ clinician reviews your case and gets you on the right treatment, privately.

The problem

Most men wait too long. Timing is the variable that matters most.

Androgenetic alopecia (male pattern baldness) affects roughly half of all men by age 50. It's driven primarily by DHT, a hormone derived from testosterone. The follicles don't die immediately; they miniaturise over time. That window is when treatment works.

The evidence for finasteride and minoxidil is strong. Used early, they slow loss significantly and often regrow some of what's been lost. Used late, results are more limited. The earlier you start, the more there is to preserve.

What we treat

Male pattern baldness

The most common form of hair loss in men. Driven by DHT and genetics. Highly treatable when caught early.

Early-stage thinning

The earlier treatment starts, the more there is to preserve. A clinician can confirm the pattern and recommend accordingly.

Hairline recession

A receding hairline is often the first sign of androgenetic alopecia. Assessed and managed as part of your treatment plan.

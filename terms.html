2. How the service works

Arua connects you with New Zealand-registered prescribers who can assess whether treatment is appropriate for you, and prescribe medication where it is. The clinical assessment is done by an independent prescriber based on the information you provide in your health questionnaire and any follow-up they require. Arua provides the platform; the prescriber provides the clinical care.

A prescriber may decline to prescribe if treatment isn't appropriate, isn't safe based on what you've told them, or if they need more information than the questionnaire provides. That's a clinical decision and it sits with them, not us. If you're declined, we'll tell you, and any payment you've made for the prescription itself will be refunded — though assessment fees may not be, depending on the model in place at the time.

Any medication prescribed to you is dispensed by a registered New Zealand pharmacy partner and delivered to the address you provide.

To use most of the service you need an account. You're responsible for keeping your login details secure and for everything that happens under your account. If you think someone else has accessed your account, tell us as soon as possible at hello@arua.co.nz.

The information you give us — in your account and especially in your health questionnaire — needs to be accurate, complete, and current. Treatment decisions are made on the basis of what you tell us. If you leave things out or aren't truthful, the prescriber can't assess you properly, and that's on you rather than us.

You can close your account at any time by contacting us. We'll retain your health information for the period required by New Zealand law (see our Privacy Policy for detail), and delete the rest within a reasonable period.

6. The clinical side — important

Arua is not a substitute for your usual GP or for emergency care. If you have a medical emergency, call 111. If you have ongoing health issues that need broader management than what we treat, see your GP.

Information on our website — articles, content library entries, FAQs — is general health information, not personal medical advice. It's intended to help you make sense of conditions and treatments at a general level. It does not replace the clinical assessment and advice you receive from a prescriber through the service.

Medications carry side effects. Before you start any treatment, read the information provided with your medication and the guidance your prescriber gives you. If you experience a serious side effect, stop the medication and contact a doctor — call 111 if it's urgent.
